Step 3 of 6 — Your Generation
Which generation are you?
This determines the entire lens of your financial advice. A first-generation earner building from zero needs a completely different roadmap than someone inheriting a foundation.
🌱
First Generation Earner
You are the first in your family to earn, invest, or build significant wealth. No financial inheritance. No template to follow. You are writing the first chapter.
Building from zero
🌿
Transition Generation
Your family had some assets or business — but no structured wealth or investment culture. You saw struggle AND some comfort. You are formalising what your parents built informally.
Formalising the foundation
🌳
Second Generation — Business Family
You inherited a business, property, or structured wealth. Your parents built the foundation. Your task is to grow, protect, and multiply what exists — and not lose it.
Growing what was built
🏛️
Third Generation or Beyond
Multi-generational wealth, established family name or business. The risk here is different — preservation, legacy, and the famous "third generation destroys wealth" pattern.
Preserving the legacy
🔄
Restarting After a Setback
You had wealth or income but lost it — business failure, health, divorce, or bad decisions. You are rebuilding with experience but without the asset base.
Rebuilding with wisdom

⚖️ The 50-30-20 Rule
A globally proven framework. 50% of take-home income for needs, 30% for wants, 20% for savings and investments. How does your spending compare?
Enter your income in the Entry tab to see your 50-30-20 breakdown.
📖 Why 50-30-20 Works
50% — Needs: Rent, food, utilities, EMIs, insurance. Non-negotiable expenses. If this exceeds 50%, your lifestyle is misaligned with your income.
30% — Wants: Dining out, entertainment, shopping, vacations. Life must be lived. But wants above 30% consistently means you are funding today at the cost of tomorrow.
20% — Savings: SIP, EPF, NPS, emergency fund. This is the number that determines your wealth trajectory. Every 1% more here compounds into significant wealth over 20 years.

📖 The Goal Planning Philosophy
Rule 1 — Name the goal specifically. "Retirement" is not a goal. "₹3 Crore retirement corpus by age 60" is a goal. Vague goals never get funded.
Rule 2 — Inflation is the hidden enemy. ₹50 lakh today for a home becomes ₹89 lakh in 10 years at 6% inflation. Always adjust for inflation.
Rule 3 — Start immediately. Waiting 1 year to start a 15-year goal increases the required monthly SIP by 8–12%. Every month of delay compounds against you.
Rule 4 — Separate goals, separate SIPs. Never mix goals in one investment. One SIP = One goal. Clarity prevents raid-and-spend behaviour.
Rule 5 — Review annually. Income grows. Priorities shift. Goals change. Recalculate every April. Adjust SIPs accordingly.

📋 Term Insurance Buying Guide
✅
Buy term, never ULIP or endowment
Pure term insurance gives the highest cover at the lowest cost. ULIPs and endowment plans mix investment and insurance — both poorly. Return of premium term plans also not worth it.
✅
Cover = 15–20× annual income minimum
At ₹10L annual income → minimum ₹1.5Cr to ₹2Cr cover. Most Indians are severely underinsured at 3–5× income.
✅
Buy before 35 — premium doubles after 40
A ₹1Cr term plan at 28 costs ₹6,000–₹8,000/year. At 40, the same plan costs ₹14,000–₹18,000/year. Health conditions after 35 may also restrict eligibility.
✅
Claim Settlement Ratio above 98%
Check IRDAI annual report. Best CSR 2024: HDFC Life 99.5%, Max Life 99.3%, ICICI Prudential 99.2%, Tata AIA 99.0%, LIC 98.5%.
✅
Never miss a premium — no grace for lapsed policy
Set auto-debit for annual premium. A lapsed term policy provides zero cover — the entire protection purpose is defeated.

📋 Health Insurance Buying Guide
✅
Minimum ₹10L individual, ₹20L family floater — metro
A single ICU admission in a tier-1 hospital costs ₹3L–₹8L today. ₹5L cover is dangerously inadequate. Always buy the highest sum insured you can afford.
✅
Super Top-up over higher base plan
₹5L base + ₹20L super top-up = ₹25L effective cover at fraction of the cost of a ₹25L base plan. HDFC ERGO, Niva Bupa, and Star Health offer the best super top-up plans.
✅
Do not rely only on employer cover
Employer health cover lapses the day you leave the job. Between jobs, you are uninsured. Buy a personal plan early when premiums are low and health is good.
✅
Buy parents' plan separately — never add to family floater
Adding senior parents to family floater drastically increases premium and exhausts the pool. Buy a separate senior citizen plan for parents — Star Senior Citizen Red Carpet or Niva Bupa Senior.

🏗️ The Protection Pyramid
Layer 1 — Emergency Fund (Foundation)
6 months of expenses in liquid form. Before any insurance, any SIP, any investment. Without this, one unexpected event wipes out all other plans.
Layer 2 — Term Life Insurance
15–20× annual income. Pure term, no frills. Protects your family's income if you are gone. This is not negotiable for anyone with dependents.
Layer 3 — Health Insurance
₹10L+ individual / ₹20L+ family. Medical inflation at 12% means today's ₹5L cover is worth ₹1.5L in purchasing power in 10 years.
Layer 4 — Critical Illness Cover
Lump sum payout on cancer, heart attack, stroke diagnosis. Covers income loss during treatment — health insurance only covers hospital bills.
Layer 5 — Personal Accident Cover
Covers permanent disability or accidental death. At ₹500–₹800/year for ₹1Cr cover — the cheapest protection available. Most people skip it.
⚠️ The ULIP Trap — Read This
What ULIP agents tell you: "It gives you insurance AND investment returns. You get the best of both worlds."
The truth: In a ULIP, of your first year premium — 20–35% goes in charges. The insurance component gives you 5–10× cover vs 15–20× from term. The investment component underperforms direct mutual funds after charges. Buy term + SIP separately. You get more cover AND more investment growth — always.

📖 The Net Worth Mindset
Track monthly, not daily. Net worth is a long game. Checking daily causes anxiety. Checking monthly builds discipline and reveals real trends.
Assets − Liabilities = Freedom. The only financial number that matters long term. Income is a flow. Net worth is the stock. Wealth is built when net worth grows faster than lifestyle.
Two ways to grow net worth: Increase assets (invest more) or decrease liabilities (repay debt). Both work. The fastest growth comes from doing both simultaneously.
Net worth doubles at predictable rates. At 12% annual growth, net worth doubles every 6 years. At 15%, every 5 years. The Rule of 72 applies to your entire balance sheet.

📊 New Tax Regime — FY 2025-26
Default regime. No deductions except 80CCD(2). Rebate u/s 87A: zero tax up to ₹12L income (₹12.75L for salaried with ₹75K standard deduction).
New regime benefit: If your total deductions under old regime are below ₹3.75L, new regime saves more tax. The break-even deduction amount is approximately ₹3.5–4L for most income levels.
📊 Old Tax Regime — FY 2025-26
Allows all deductions. Beneficial only if total deductions exceed ₹3.5–4L. Rebate u/s 87A: zero tax up to ₹5L taxable income.

📅 Tax Harvesting — How It Works
Step 1 — Check your unrealised LTCG
Log in to your MF platform (Zerodha, Groww, Kuvera). Go to P&L report. See total LTCG unrealised.
Step 2 — Book up to ₹1.25L every March
Sell units with highest unrealised LTCG first. Stop at ₹1.25L gain. This is completely tax free under Sec 112A.
Step 3 — Immediately rebuy the same fund
Rebuy the same units within 1–2 days. Your cost basis resets to today's NAV. Future gains start from a higher base.
Step 4 — Repeat every year
₹1.25L harvested per year × 20 years = ₹25L in tax-free gains. At 30% slab, this saves approximately ₹7.5L in total tax over 20 years.

📖 The 4% Safe Withdrawal Rule
The global benchmark for retirement sustainability: withdraw no more than 4% of your corpus per year. At this rate, a well-invested portfolio historically lasts 30+ years.
Indian adaptation: Use 3–3.5% withdrawal rate due to higher inflation (6% vs 2-3% in the West). This means you need 28–33× your annual expenses as retirement corpus.
Formula: Annual expenses × 33 = your target retirement corpus. At ₹80,000/month expenses → ₹96L/year → target corpus = ₹3.17 Cr.

📋 Key Optimisation Levers
Employer NPS 80CCD(2): Ask HR to put 10% of Basic into NPS as employer contribution. No upper limit. Fully deductible. This alone can save ₹30,000–₹60,000/year in tax for a ₹15–20L CTC.
HRA structuring: Ensure HRA is at least 40–50% of Basic. If you pay rent, this is the single largest exemption available.
LTA (Leave Travel Allowance): ₹30,000–₹50,000 tax free if you travel domestically twice in a 4-year block.
Flexi benefits: Telephone/internet allowance, food coupons (Sodexo), medical reimbursement — small amounts that add up to ₹20,000–₹40,000 tax free annually.
